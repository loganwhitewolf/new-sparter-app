<objective>
Lay the additive schema foundation for the Expense Group model (ADR 0017): two new tables
(`expenseGroup`, `expenseGroupMembership`) with no persisted aggregate columns, applied via a
real migration, plus the Zod validation schemas the merge and rename actions will use in the
next wave.

Purpose: every later plan in this phase (services, DAL composition, UI) depends on these tables
existing in the live dev database and on `MergeExpensesSchema`/`RenameExpenseGroupSchema` being
importable. Getting the constraint shape right here (especially the expense-can-only-join-one-group
rule) prevents a data-integrity bug that would otherwise surface much later.
Output: `lib/db/schema.ts` additions, a generated+applied SQL migration, and two new Zod schemas.
</objective>

<assumption_delta_decision>
noun: Expense (unchanged primary identity)
decision: add-alongside ŌĆö Expense Group is a new grouping entity layered above Expense, never a
replacement or promotion of it.
rationale: ADR 0017 explicitly rejects promoting the group to primary identity. Expense keeps its
hash-keyed identity, its Tier 2 classification-history key, and its re-import dedup behavior
completely untouched. The group is reversible-by-construction precisely because it owns no data
that would need to be un-migrated on dissolve (Phase 66 dissolve is just deleting expenseGroup +
expenseGroupMembership rows ŌĆö nothing on `expense` or `transaction` ever changes).
</assumption_delta_decision>
